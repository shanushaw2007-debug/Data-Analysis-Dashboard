# Data-Analysis-Dashbord
Project Description  
This project focuses on analyzing employee data to understand workforce patterns, attrition behavior, and key HR metrics. Using Power BI, a fully interactive dashboard was created based on the HRAnalytics.csv file. The dashboard helps HR departments make data‑driven decisions regarding employee retention, job satisfaction, salary structure, and demographic distribution.

The main goal of this project is to transform raw HR data into meaningful insights through visualizations that are simple to understand yet detailed enough to support decision‑making.

Dependencies / Tools Used  
• Power BI Desktop (for data cleaning, modeling, and visualization)  
• Power Query Editor (for data transformation)  
• CSV File (HRAnalytics.csv)  
• DAX Measures (for calculations like attrition rate, averages, totals)

Data Source  
• Primary Source: HR_Analytics.csv  
This file contains employee information such as Age, Gender, Job Role, Salary, Education, Department, Attrition, Experience, and more.

Data Cleaning & Transformations  
Several filters and transformation steps were applied to clean and organize the raw data:

• Removed duplicates  
• Replaced missing values  
• Grouped data to calculate totals (e.g., group by JobRole, AgeGroup, SalarySlab)  
• Created calculated columns  
• Applied measures like:  
  - Count of employees  
  - Count of attrition  
  - Sum of salary  
  - Average age  
  - Average monthly income  
• Converted data types (text, number, categorical)  
• Created Age Groups (18‑25, 26‑35, 36‑45, 46‑55, 55+)  
• Created Salary Slabs (Upto 5k, 5k‑10k, 10k‑15k, 15k+)  

All these transformations helped build a clean and powerful dataset ready for visualization.

What the Data Shows (Summary of Insights)  
The HR data reveals important patterns such as:

• Total Employees: 1470  
• Total Attrition: 237  
• Attrition Rate: 16.1%  
• Average Employee Age: 37  
• Average Salary: 6.5k  
• Average Experience: 7 years  
• Most attrition happens in the age group 26‑35  
• Highest attrition in job roles like Laboratory Technician and Sales Executive  
• Lower salary slabs show the highest attrition numbers  
• Life Sciences and Medical are the most common education backgrounds

These insights help HR teams understand where attrition is high and what areas require attention.

Dashboard Explanation (Simple and Elaborate)  
The Power BI dashboard is designed to give a 360‑degree view of employee behavior and HR metrics. Each visual tells a story:

• Employee Overview Cards  
Shows total number of employees, total attrition, attrition rate, average age, average salary, and average experience.

• Attrition by Gender  
A pie chart showing how many males and females left the company.

• Attrition by Education  
A donut chart showing which educational backgrounds have higher attrition.  
Most attrition is among employees with Life Sciences and Medical degrees.

• Attrition by Age Group  
A bar chart explaining which age group has maximum attrition.  
26‑35 age group shows the highest employee exits.

• Attrition by Salary Slab  
Shows which salary levels have most attrition.  
Lower salary groups show highest employee turnover.

• Attrition by Years at Company  
A line graph showing how years of service affect attrition.  
Most employees leave within the first few years.

• Attrition by Job Role  
Shows top job roles where attrition is highest.  
Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists contribute heavily.

• Department‑wise Count  
Displays employee distribution among  
• Human Resources  
• Research & Development  
• Sales  

This helps understand departmental workloads.

• Job Role Table  
Shows attrition count across different roles and job levels.

Each visual connects to HR problems like retention, salary dissatisfaction, job instability, and department‑wise performance.

Purpose of the Dashboard  
• To help HR understand why employees are leaving  
• To identify high‑risk employee groups  
• To improve salary, job satisfaction, and workplace conditions  
• To support data‑based decision-making  
• To make HR analytics simpler and more effective
